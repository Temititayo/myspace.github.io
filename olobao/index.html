Welcome to My Academic and Professional Website

I'm a lecturer in Peace and Conflict Studies with interests in data, technology, and traditional conflict resolution, as well as a chartered mediator, security and development practitioner, and a student of climate impact.

I explore the use of computational and quantitative methods in social science, particularly in conflict and development studies. I also explore the use of agent-based modeling, artificial societies and machine learning for policy transformation and fieldwork in the Global South.

Part of my interests include engineered multi-layered system integrating calibrated historical data, causal dynamics, and even geodesic curvature analogies with implications for time-like structures, as well as the use of differential equations, geodesic paths, and sensitivity analysis to model resentment and conflict intensity. I am also interested in using closed timelike curves (CTCs) as proxies for intractable conflict cycles.

Welcome to my multi-layered and transdisciplinary world for understanding the role of computational technology in the development of complex social systems!
