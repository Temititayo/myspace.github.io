Welcome to My Space

I'm a researcher in Peace, Conflict and Development Studies with interests in data, technology, and traditional conflict resolution, as well as innovative development solutions. I am also a chartered mediator, security and development practitioner, and a student of the impacts the changes in climate trajectory make.

Specifically, I explore the use of computational and quantitative methods in the social sciences, particularly in conflict and development studies. I also explore the use of agent-based modeling, artificial societies and machine learning for policy transformation and fieldwork in the Global South.

Part of my interests include engineered multi-layered system integrating calibrated historical data, causal dynamics, and even geodesic curvature analogies with implications for time-like structures. My interests also gears towards the use of differential equations, geodesic paths, and sensitivity analysis to model resentment and conflict intensity. I am very interested in using closed timelike curves (CTCs) as proxies for modeling intractable conflict cycles and climate change impact cycles.

Welcome to my multi-layered and transdisciplinary world for understanding the role of computational technology in the development of complex social systems!
